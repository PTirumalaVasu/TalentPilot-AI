## Scenario Entry Context

**User Situation:** Casey is an individual contributor at SAILS Software who has just received an assigned skill from Rita in TalentPilot-AI.

**Mental State:**
- Hope: See the assigned skill with relevant content ready to watch, and know they can resume exactly where they left off next time.
- Worry: Losing their place in the video or the system not saving progress, getting no credit for work.

**Discovery Method:** Casey receives a Slack notification or email from Rita: "Data Visualization Fundamentals assigned to you in TalentPilot-AI." Casey clicks the link or opens the bookmark.

---

## Page Overview

**Page Purpose:**
Casey sees the assigned skill card with a single, human-approved, AI-surfaced content recommendation already pre-selected — no search box, no browsing required. Thumbnail + duration + "play" button are immediately visible. Casey clicks to start watching.

**Entry Context:**
Casey arrives from Slack notification or browser bookmark and sees the assignment in TalentPilot-AI.

**Exit Action:**
Casey clicks the play button (or video thumbnail) to start watching the recommended content.

---

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

**Interaction 1: Assignment Card Loads**
- Casey opens TalentPilot-AI and the assignment card is immediately visible.
- **Key UX moment:** Casey does not need to search for content, browse options, or make decisions. The system has already surfaced one appropriate, human-approved video. This reduces friction to zero — Casey's only decision is "do I watch this?" not "what should I watch?"

**Interaction 2: Hover on "Approved" Badge (Optional)**
- Casey hovers over the "✓ Approved" badge.
- Tooltip appears: "This content was reviewed and approved by Rita before being recommended to you."
- **Key UX moment:** Reinforces that this is human-vetted, not auto-generated YouTube search spam. Builds trust in the recommendation.

**Interaction 3: Click Play (Exit Action)**
- Casey clicks the play button or clicks the video thumbnail.
- Player launches, video begins at 0:00.
- Exit: Navigate to 02.2 Resume/Continue Watching (but first, Casey watches the video and may close the tab).

## Content Rules

**Recommended Content Display:**
- ✅ Always show: thumbnail, title, duration, source, approval status, brief description.
- ✅ Never show raw search results; show exactly one curated recommendation per skill.
- ✅ Always label as "Recommended" or "Suggested," never "Search Results."
- ✅ Always include human-approval provenance (✓ Approved, Reviewed by [name], etc.).
- ✅ Brief description should be learning-outcome focused, not just a transcript snippet.

**Copy Tone:**
- Warm and encouraging (employee-facing, not HR-facing).
- Frictionless: "Here's what we recommend" not "Choose from these 47 options."
- No compliance language or mandatory framing — treat as helpful, not a chore.

---

## Design Constraints

- **No search box on this page:** Casey should never feel the need to search. If alternatives are needed, hide behind a small link ("View alternatives").
- **Video player**: Industry-standard (YouTube IFrame player or similar). Must support:
  - Play/pause controls
  - Progress bar with current-time indicator
  - Full-screen mode
  - Volume control
  - Speed adjustment (optional, but nice-to-have)
- **Watch-position capture:** Implemented via `sendBeacon` on tab close to ensure even abrupt exits save the last known position.
- **Responsive:** Desktop-primary; video player should scale to available viewport width.
- **Performance:** Page + video player must load in < 3 seconds.

---

## Integration Notes for Build

- **Content source:** YouTube IFrame API with polling-based `getCurrentTime()` to capture watch-% continuously.
- **Watch-position persistence:** Every 5–10 seconds during playback, persist `{watch_position, timestamp}` to the backend. On tab close, flush the last known position via `sendBeacon`.
- **Next step trigger:** When Casey closes the tab (or navigates away), the tab-close event captures the position and signals to Rita's dashboard to update the row.

## Scenario Entry Context

**User Situation:** Rita is at her desk on Wednesday morning when a project lead Slack-messages asking "who can we staff on the Q3 skills initiative?" She needs to answer this readiness question in minutes with confidence.

**Mental State:**
- Hope: Open the dashboard and instantly trust which rows are verified so she can answer without a second thought.
- Worry: The dashboard looks professional but hides stale data, causing her to make a wrong staffing call.

**Discovery Method:** Rita clicks the TalentPilot-AI bookmark in her browser and opens the Assignment Dashboard.

---

## Page Overview

**Page Purpose:** 
Rita scans the skill rows and immediately sees provenance labels (Verified · 92% / Self-reported · 14 days / Assigned · Awaiting / Needs Attention) next to each employee name; she spots inconsistencies and identifies which rows genuinely need attention vs. which are verified.

**Entry Context:**
Rita arrives from her browser bookmark — this is her daily starting point for readiness checks.

**Exit Action:**
Rita clicks on a stale row (drill-down arrow) to see the provenance explanation before making her readiness call.

## Page Structure & Content

### Header
- Logo + project name "TalentPilot-AI"
- User context: "Rita · HR/L&D Admin"
- Quick-access buttons: [Assignments] [Dashboard] [Account]

### Main Content: Skill Assignment Grid

**Grid Columns (left to right):**
1. **Employee Name** — clickable to Employee Profile
2. **Assigned Skill** — the must-do skill name
3. **Status + Provenance Label** — the critical column:
   - `Verified · 92% watched, 2 hours ago` (green text, auto-captured video signal)
   - `Self-reported · Not updated in 14 days` (amber text, non-video, stale)
   - `Assigned · Awaiting first watch` (gray text, no signal yet)
   - `Needs Attention · Inconsistent signal` (red text, flagged for review)
4. **Completion % or Days Since Update** — visual progress bar or timestamp
5. **Drill-Down Arrow** — click to see provenance reasoning

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

**Interaction 1: Visual Scan for Inconsistencies**
- Rita lands on the dashboard and immediately scans the Status column.
- Her eye is drawn to the provenance labels: she instantly sees which rows are verified (green) vs. self-reported (amber) vs. awaiting (gray) vs. flagged (red).
- She spots the inconsistency: "Casey and Morgan are verified, but Jordan and Sam are stale — one is 21 days old, the other 18 days."
- **Key UX moment:** The mixed-trust labeling makes the inconsistency immediately obvious. If all rows looked uniformly gray/professional, she would have to read text carefully to notice the difference. Here, the color + text pairing makes it scannable.

**Interaction 2: Hover on Status Label for Tooltip (Optional)**
- Rita hovers over "Self-reported · Not updated in 14 days" and sees a small tooltip: "Self-reported data is employee-entered, not auto-captured. This row has not been updated in 14 days."
- This reinforces the distinction and reduces doubt.

**Interaction 3: Click to Filter (Exit Action)**
- Rita decides to focus only on the rows that genuinely need attention.
- She clicks the **[Needs Attention Filter]** button at the top right.
- Exit: Navigate to 01.2 Needs Attention Filter view.

## Content Rules

**Provenance Labeling Non-Negotiables:**
- ✅ Always pair status text with a visual treatment (color + icon, not color-only).
- ✅ Never abbreviate provenance language. Write "Verified," not "V." Write "Self-reported," not "SR."
- ✅ Always include freshness: "2 hours ago," not just "Verified."
- ✅ "Needs Attention" must state the reason briefly: "Stale self-report," "Inconsistent signal," "Not started yet."

**Copy Tone:**
- Clear, matter-of-fact, no fluff.
- HR-facing copy is factual and confident, never playful.

---

## Design Constraints

- **Responsive:** Desktop-primary (no mobile version needed; Rita always checks from her desk).
- **Performance:** Dashboard must load in < 2 seconds (real-time feel expected).
- **Real-time updates:** If Casey finishes a video while Rita is viewing the dashboard, the row should update automatically (or show a subtle refresh indicator).

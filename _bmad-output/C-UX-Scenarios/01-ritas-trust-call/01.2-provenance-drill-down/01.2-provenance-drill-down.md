## Page Overview

**Page Purpose:**
Rita views the raw data (watch-%, timestamp) underlying a dashboard row's provenance label, confirming exactly what signal the system captured and when.

**Entry Context:**
Rita clicked on a stale row's drill-down arrow from the Assignment Dashboard (01.1). The drill-down modal or side panel opens, showing the detailed signal.

**Exit Action:**
Rita closes the drill-down (clicks X, Escape, or clicks outside the modal) and returns to the Assignment Dashboard, having seen the raw data she needed. Scenario complete.

## Page Structure & Content

### Drill-Down Container (Modal or Side Panel)

**Header:**
- Row identifier: "Employee: Jordan | Skill: Data Visualization Fundamentals"
- Close button [X]

**Content Sections:**

#### Section 1: Provenance Summary
- **Label:** "Self-reported · Not updated in 21 days"
- **Type:** Self-reported (non-video skill, employee-entered)
- **Last Update:** 21 days ago (specific date/time: "2026-06-17 at 3:42 PM")

#### Section 2: Raw Signal Data
- **Assignment Date:** 2026-06-17
- **Assignment by:** Rita the Referee
- **Content Link:** [Data Visualization Fundamentals — Video or Doc]
- **Watch Activity (if video):** None recorded
- **Last Self-Report Entry:** "In progress — 40% estimated" (date: 2026-06-17)
- **Days Since Last Activity:** 21 days

#### Section 3: What This Means
- Plain-language explanation: "This skill was assigned 21 days ago. No video watch activity has been recorded since assignment. The employee's last self-reported update said '40% estimated,' but that entry was made on the same day as assignment and hasn't been updated since. The row is marked 'needs attention' because the signal is stale and we can't confirm current progress."

#### Section 4: Actions (Optional, Context-Dependent)
- **[Close]** button — Rita dismisses the drill-down and returns to the dashboard

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

**Interaction 1: Drill-Down Opens**
- Rita clicks the drill-down arrow on Jordan's row.
- Modal/side panel slides in or appears, showing the provenance details.
- **Key UX moment:** The raw data is immediately visible — no additional clicks needed to see watch-% or timestamp.

**Interaction 2: Rita Scans the Raw Data**
- Rita reads the Assignment Date, Last Update, and plain-language explanation.
- She confirms: "Assigned 21 days ago, no watch activity, last self-report was 40% on day one."
- She now has all the information needed to make a judgment call about Jordan's readiness.

**Interaction 3: Close and Return**
- Rita clicks [Close] or [X] or presses Escape.
- Drill-down closes, returning to the Assignment Dashboard (01.1).
- **Scenario Complete** ✓

## Content Rules

**Raw Data Display:**
- ✅ Always show Assignment Date, Last Activity Date/Time, and Signal Type.
- ✅ Include plain-language explanation of what the signal means (e.g., "No video watch activity recorded").
- ✅ If self-reported, show the last self-reported value and when it was entered.
- ✅ Never hide staleness; always state it explicitly (e.g., "Not updated in 21 days").

**Copy Tone:**
- Factual and precise.
- Explain what happened (assignment date, last update) and what it means (no current signal, stale, etc.).
- No interpretation or judgment — just the facts Rita needs.

---

## Design Constraints

- **Modal vs. Side Panel:** Designer choice; either works. Modal (centered pop-up) emphasizes the data; side panel (right-slide) allows Rita to compare with the dashboard row behind it if desired.
- **Responsive:** Desktop-primary; no mobile version needed.
- **Quick Close:** Escape key and X button should both close instantly — Rita should never feel trapped in this view.

# Casey the Continuer - Secondary Persona

> Secondary target - the person whose passive, honest engagement is what keeps Rita's dashboard trustworthy

**Priority:** SECONDARY 💼
**Role in Flywheel:** Generates the verified signal simply by doing what they'd want to do anyway (watch assigned content and pick up where they left off) — the trust loop closes through Casey's convenience, not their compliance
**Created:** 2026-07-08

---

## Profile Summary

**Casey isn't lazy about updating the shared sheet — updating it just has zero payoff for them, so it's the first thing that gets dropped when anything else is competing for their attention.**

Casey is an individual contributor at SAILS Software, assigned must-do skills and courses by Rita. Today, they self-report progress into the same shared spreadsheet Rita maintains — an easy-to-deprioritize chore that exists purely for someone else's benefit, with no personal reward loop of its own. Casey matters to TalentPilot-AI's success because they're the actual source of the signal Rita depends on: if Casey's engagement isn't honest and frictionless, there's no trustworthy data for Rita to see, no matter how good the dashboard looks.

## Background

### Education & Career Path

**Learning Journey:** Casey has picked up skills the way most working professionals do — some formal training, a lot of on-the-job learning, and periodic assigned courses whenever the org identifies a skill gap.

**Current Role:** An individual contributor at SAILS Software, currently juggling active project work alongside whatever skills/courses HR has assigned as "must-do."

**Career Pattern:** Steady, not dramatic — Casey isn't actively job-hunting or disengaged; they simply have a limited, contested amount of attention each day, and self-reporting progress on a shared spreadsheet loses that contest almost every time.

## Current Situation

### Professional Reality

**The Daily Struggle:**
- Assigned must-do skills/courses by Rita, with no personal system for tracking where they left off
- Expected to self-report progress into the same shared sheet Rita uses — a chore disconnected from any actual benefit to them
- No consistent way to resume video content exactly where they stopped — depends on manually remembering or re-scrubbing through video
- Competing priorities (real project deadlines) always win against "go update the tracking sheet"

**Skills & Tools:**
- Comfortable with everyday consumer apps — expects a "continue watching" experience the way Netflix or Spotify already deliver it
- No specialized learning-platform experience; whatever HR assigns is whatever they use
- Skill gap isn't about learning ability — it's about there being no natural on-ramp back into content they already started

**The Zero-Payoff Gap:**
- Self-reporting exists entirely for someone else's (Rita's) benefit, with no return for Casey
- Updating a status field about your own progress isn't "real work," so it's the easiest thing to defer indefinitely
- What's blocking honest signal: there's no reason to keep the sheet current unless someone chases you, and being chased itself feels bad
- What Casey actually needs: a system that tracks their progress *for* them, as a byproduct of something they'd do anyway (watching the content)

## Psychological Profile

### Personality & Motivations

**Core Identity:**
- Casey isn't avoiding responsibility — they're avoiding an activity (self-reporting) that has no connection to anything they personally care about
- Deep motivation: make progress on assigned learning without it costing extra administrative effort on top of the learning itself
- Belief system: if a tool remembers where you left off without being asked, that's basic decency in software design — it's the bar consumer apps cleared years ago

**Work Style:**
- Casey engages with content in bursts — a few minutes here, interrupted, back later — which makes "exactly where I left off" resume behavior essential, not a nice-to-have
- Values low-friction entry points: if finding the right content or resuming it takes more than a couple of clicks, they'll postpone it
- Makes decisions about what to do next based on immediate context (what's due, what's already open) rather than a structured learning plan
- Communication preference: quiet nudges over demands — a gentle "pick up where you left off" prompt lands better than a compliance reminder

## Driving Forces

### ✅ Top 3 Positive Drivers (What They Want)

**1. Resume exactly where they left off, consumer-app style**

- Casey wants to close a video mid-way through and come back later to exactly that point, the way Netflix or Spotify already works
- Why it matters: re-finding their place manually is friction that makes them less likely to return to the content at all
- What success looks like: one click back into a "Continue Watching" card lands them at the exact second they stopped
- **TalentPilot-AI Promise:** The same watch-position write that updates HR's dashboard also powers the resume card — Casey gets pixel-accurate resume for free, as a side effect of a system built for someone else's trust problem.

**2. Never have to self-report progress on assigned video content**

- Casey wants their actual behavior (watching) to be the entire extent of their obligation — no separate step to log or confirm it
- Why it matters: removing the reporting step removes the only part of "learning" that felt like unpaid administrative work
- What success looks like: they watch a video, close the tab, and that's the whole interaction — no form, no status field, no reminder
- **TalentPilot-AI Promise:** Video watch-% is auto-captured, never self-reported — Casey's participation in the trust pipeline is entirely passive and effortless.

**3. Find the right content fast, without guessing what to watch**

- Casey wants to open TalentPilot-AI after being assigned a skill and immediately see relevant content, not a blank search box
- Why it matters: uncertainty about what to watch is its own friction, separate from (and additive to) the reporting-chore friction they're already avoiding
- What success looks like: an AI-surfaced video/doc/web recommendation appears inline the moment a skill is assigned, no extra searching required
- **TalentPilot-AI Promise:** An AI content-discovery assistant matches assigned skills to relevant video/doc/website content automatically, collapsing "find something to watch" into the same moment as "get assigned something."

---

### ❌ Top 3 Negative Drivers (What They Fear)

**1. Losing their place and having to re-scrub through a video to find it**

- Casey's most immediate frustration is re-watching or fast-forwarding through content they've already seen just to get back to where they stopped
- Why this frustrates them: it's wasted time on top of time they're already carving out of a busy day, and it makes returning to unfinished content feel like a penalty rather than a continuation
- What failure looks like: they abandon a half-finished video entirely because picking it back up feels like more effort than it's worth
- **TalentPilot-AI Answer:** Watch-position is captured continuously (with `sendBeacon` on tab close ensuring the position is saved even on an abrupt exit) — there's no scenario where closing the tab loses their spot.

**2. Being watched/tracked in a way that feels invasive rather than helpful**

- Casey may feel uneasy if being told "the system detected you watched 73%" reads as surveillance rather than a convenience feature working in the background
- Why this matters: if the tracking feels creepy instead of useful, Casey may disengage from the content entirely rather than feel monitored
- What failure looks like: Casey avoids watching assigned content at their desk, or feels self-conscious about pausing/skipping, because they sense they're being evaluated moment-to-moment
- **TalentPilot-AI Answer:** Employee-facing copy frames tracking entirely in terms of personal benefit ("Pick up where you left off — 8 min remaining") — never in terms of surveillance language — and the underlying signal is scoped narrowly to watch position, not granular behavioral judgment.

**3. Doing the work and getting no credit for it because the system doesn't register it correctly**

- Casey fears watching an entire assigned video only to have it silently fail to register as complete — leaving them exposed to a status they don't deserve, with no way to prove otherwise
- Why this matters: unlike the old spreadsheet (where at least Casey controlled their own self-reported entry), an auto-capture system that glitches removes Casey's ability to correct the record themselves
- What failure looks like: Casey genuinely finishes a video, but a tracking gap makes it look like they didn't, and HR has no reason to think otherwise
- **TalentPilot-AI Answer:** The polling-based YouTube IFrame API approach captures granular watch-% (not just a binary complete/incomplete), and the dashboard explicitly labels ambiguous signal as "Needs Attention" rather than a silent false negative — giving Rita a reason to double-check rather than accept an incorrect gap at face value.

## Validation Strategy

### What Casey Needs to See About TalentPilot-AI

**1. Proof the resume mechanic actually works, immediately**
- The first time Casey returns to a paused video, it must land exactly where they left off — this is the single moment that either builds or breaks their trust in the whole system
- Proof point: a visible "Continue Watching" card with an accurate progress indicator, not a generic "resume" button that guesses

**2. Confirmation that there's genuinely nothing left to self-report**
- No lingering status field, no "confirm your progress" prompt layered on top of the auto-capture
- Trust signal: the interface simply doesn't ask them anything about their own progress

**3. Content that's actually relevant to what they were assigned**
- If the AI-surfaced content feels off-target, Casey will lose confidence in the discovery feature and revert to searching manually
- Proof point: the first assigned skill produces a genuinely watchable, on-topic recommendation

**4. Language that reads as personally useful, not evaluative**
- Employee-facing copy should read like a streaming app's resume prompt, not a compliance system's status report
- Trust signal: "Pick up where you left off — 8 min remaining," not "Your watch status: 73%"

## Conversion Path

### How Casey Validates TalentPilot-AI

**Phase 1: Discovery**
- Rita assigns a must-do skill; Casey receives it as an assignment inside TalentPilot-AI rather than a spreadsheet row

**Phase 2: Evaluation**
- Casey watches the first piece of AI-surfaced content, gets interrupted, and later checks whether "Continue Watching" actually resumes at the right spot

**Phase 3: Adoption**
- Once resume works reliably, Casey stops thinking about the tracking mechanism at all — it becomes invisible infrastructure, the way Netflix's resume feature is invisible until it's missing

**Phase 4: Advocacy**
- Casey mentions, informally, that they no longer have to update any tracking sheet for video content — the kind of low-key endorsement that reinforces Rita's own confidence in the system, without Casey needing to actively "champion" anything

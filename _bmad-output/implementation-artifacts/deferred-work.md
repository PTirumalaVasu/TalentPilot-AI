## Deferred from: consolidated code review of Stories 1.2, 1.3, 1.4 (2026-07-09)

A full re-review across all three stories together (Blind Hunter, Edge Case Hunter, plus a dedicated cross-story consistency pass) found **no new bugs and no cross-story drift** — the cross-story consistency check explicitly confirmed claim-key alignment through the full login → JWT → dependency chain, correct layering (Story 1.2's `get_current_token_payload` doesn't touch role/user_id; Story 1.3's `get_current_user` composes on top; Story 1.4 uses both correctly), and that Story 1.3's repository-hard-scoping carve-out is still accurate (still stubs). Most findings from the two adversarial layers turned out to be duplicates of items already logged above, matches of the epic's own literal AC text (e.g. the `INVALID_ROLE` message echoing the claim value back — that's spec'd, not a bug), or premised on a production deployment this project doesn't have (architecture AR-15: local-only, no production target) — those are not re-listed here. Two items are genuinely new:

- **`SameSite=Lax` on the session cookie will block it on cross-origin requests once a real frontend exists on a different origin than the API** [backend/app/auth/service.py] — cannot be meaningfully tested or fixed yet since no frontend exists; `ALLOWED_ORIGINS` already carries a "fill in once frontend build/serve target is decided" placeholder from Story 1.1. Revisit together once frontend work begins — may need `SameSite=None; Secure` for a cross-origin split, or same-origin serving to sidestep the issue entirely.
- **Cookie `Max-Age` (`set_session_cookie`, Story 1.2) and the JWT's own `exp` claim (`create_access_token`, Story 1.1) are two independently-computed encodings of the same session lifetime**, with no shared-clock guarantee between them [backend/app/auth/service.py, backend/app/core/security.py] — negligible in practice (both computed microseconds apart within the same synchronous request path), but worth knowing if session-lifetime precision is ever tightened or these two calls end up separated across an async boundary.

Also explicitly re-confirmed as correct, not gaps: `get_current_user`/`request.state.current_user` are unused today because nothing exists yet to protect (`assignments`/`content`/`dashboard` routers are still stubs) — Story 1.6 and Epic 2-5 are where this gets wired in, not a regression; no logout endpoint exists because that's Story 1.5, the very next story in the backlog.

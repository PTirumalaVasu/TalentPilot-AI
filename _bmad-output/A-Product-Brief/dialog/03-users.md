## Reflection Checkpoint

**Agent Synthesis:**
- HR checks daily, not weekly/ad hoc — the dashboard needs to feel truly live, not just "fresher than a spreadsheet." Even a day or two of staleness would be noticeable and erode trust.
- "Resigned," not "tolerable" — HR has given up trying to fix the spreadsheet process; this is a stronger validation of the premise (real appetite for something better) than passive inertia would be.

**User Response:** Confirmed first try — "yes."

---

## Primary User: HR Admin

- **Role:** HR/L&D admin at SAILS Software, manages skill assignments and judges project-readiness
- **Daily behavior (confirmed):** Opens the shared Excel sheet **daily** to add/update must-do skills/courses per employee; periodically reviews the status column to judge readiness; chases employees who haven't self-reported
- **Frustrations:** Doesn't trust the status column — depends on someone else remembering to update it; worn down by chasing; anxious making real staffing calls on data they suspect is stale
- **Emotional state toward current process:** Resigned, not tolerant — has given up trying to improve the spreadsheet process, not just quietly accepting it
- **Goals:** Know who's ready for a project without manual check-ins or chasing; a single, live source of truth
- **Current solution:** Shared Excel spreadsheet, checked daily

## Secondary User: Employee

- **Role:** Individual contributor assigned must-do skills/courses by HR
- **Daily behavior (mostly inferred, not confirmed via direct interview):** Self-reports own progress/status into the same shared sheet — an easy-to-deprioritize chore with no personal payoff
- **Frustrations:** Updating the sheet isn't "real work"; mild guilt/avoidance about not updating; no reward loop
- **Goals:** Find the right learning content fast; resume where they left off, consumer-app style (Netflix/Spotify pattern)
- **Current solution:** Same shared spreadsheet for self-reporting; generic/unfiltered content search for finding learning material

---

## Design Implication Carried Forward

Because HR checks **daily**, "live" is a real, load-bearing product requirement — not a nice-to-have. Freshness indicators and the "Needs Attention" filter (from the Trust/Freshness Dashboard concept) need to reflect near-real-time state, not batch-updated snapshots, or the core trust mechanic breaks against actual usage frequency.
